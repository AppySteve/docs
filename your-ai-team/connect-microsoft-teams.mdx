Appy.AI works directly inside Microsoft Teams, so your team can message your AI agents without leaving the tools they already use. This guide walks through the full setup process, from installing the app to sending your first message.

The setup takes about 10 minutes and requires a Microsoft Teams administrator for the first few steps. Once the app is installed, any user in your organization can start chatting with Appy.AI.

## Prerequisites

Before you begin, make sure you have:

- An Appy.AI account with an active organization (visit [https://builder.appy.ai/swarm/signup](https://builder.appy.ai/swarm/signup))
- Microsoft Global administrator access (for the installation and consent steps)

## Start the connection flow in [Appy.AI](http://Appy.AI)

1. Log in to your Appy.AI account at [https://builder.appy.ai]
2. You'll see the **Connect Microsoft Teams** wizard, which will guide you through these steps.

# 1. Install Appy through Microsoft Teams Admin

## Enable custom app uploads

A Teams admin needs to ensure that custom app uploads are allowed in your organization. This setting controls whether users can add apps that aren't published in the Microsoft Teams app store.

<Frame>
  ![Screenshot 2026 04 06 At 3 49 51 PM](/images/Screenshot-2026-04-06-at-3.49.51-PM.png)
</Frame>

1. Go to the [Microsoft Teams admin center](https://admin.teams.microsoft.com).
2. In the left sidebar, navigate to **Teams apps** \> **Setup policies**.
3. Select the policy that applies to your organization — typically **Global (Org-wide default)**.
4. Make sure **Upload custom apps** is toggled **On**.
   <Frame>
     ![Screenshot 2026 04 06 At 3 51 51 PM](/images/Screenshot-2026-04-06-at-3.51.51-PM.png)
   </Frame>

<Info>
  This setting may already be enabled for your organization.
</Info>

## Upload the app in Microsoft Teams admin center

Now a Teams admin needs to download the [Appy.ai](http://Appy.ai) app package and upload it to teams.

1. Download the[ Appy.AI app package](https://opkyduq0pk8jvf0d.public.blob.vercel-storage.com/appyai-teams-sideload.zip). This will save a small zip file to your computer. **Do not unzip the file.**
2. Go to the [Microsoft Teams admin center](https://admin.teams.microsoft.com).
   <Frame>
     ![Screenshot 2026 04 06 At 4 00 29 PM](/images/Screenshot-2026-04-06-at-4.00.29-PM.png)
   </Frame>
3. In the left sidebar, navigate to **Teams apps** \> **Manage apps**.
4. Click the **Actions** dropdown in the top-right corner and select **Upload new app**.
5. In the dialog that appears, click **Upload**
6. Select the downloaded zip file (named something like `appyai-teams-sideload.zip`) and click **Open**.
   <Frame>
     ![Screenshot 2026 04 06 At 4 06 03 PM](/images/Screenshot-2026-04-06-at-4.06.03-PM.png)
   </Frame>
7. Wait a moment for the upload to process. You'll see a **New app added** confirmation when it's complete.
8. Search for "Appy" in the Manage apps list to verify the app is there.
9. Click **Users and Groups**
10. Click **Install App**, and select Install for Everyone or specified groups. This ensures your team members will be able to message Appy.

# 2. Grant Admin Consent

## Grant admin permissions

1. Open the [Microsoft permissions consent screen]
   <Frame>
     <img
       src="/images/image-2.png"

2. Review the permissions that Appy.AI is requesting. These include reading and sending channel messages, creating chats, and reading user profile information.
3. Click **Accept** to grant the permissions for your organization.

<Warning>
  This consent must be granted by a Global administrator. The permissions apply organization-wide — no other users will be prompted to review them separately.
</Warning>

# 3. Add the app

## Open the app in Microsoft Teams

Now that the app is installed and permissions are granted, any user in your organization can chat with Appy.AI.


## Tips for using Appy.AI in Teams

**Use direct messages for the best experience.** We recommend chatting with Appy.AI through direct messages rather than in public channels. Direct messages support file uploads and provide a smoother experience overall.

## Troubleshooting

**The app doesn't appear in Teams after uploading.** Try signing out of Teams and signing back in. The app cache can take a few minutes to update. Make sure the "Upload custom apps" setting is enabled in your setup policies.

**Permission errors during admin consent.** The person granting permissions must be a Microsoft Teams administrator with global admin privileges. If you're unsure whether you have the right access, check with your IT team.
