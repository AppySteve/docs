Your AI team starts ready to work, but gets better the more it knows about your business. Teach them your preferences, create new specialists, and scale your team as you grow.

## Refining agents through conversation

The easiest way to customize your AI team is just to tell them about your business. No forms to fill out, no settings to configure — just share context in Slack like you would with a new hire.

Here are some examples:

- "Our brand voice is casual and friendly — never corporate"
- "We always include a CTA at the end of blog posts"
- "Our target audience is small business owners in health and wellness"
- "When writing outreach emails, always mention our free trial"
- "We use AP style for all written content"

Appy.AI remembers these preferences and applies them to future work. The more context you share, the better your results get.

## Creating new agents

Need a specialist that doesn't exist yet? Just describe the role you need and Appy.AI creates it for you.

Try something like:

- "I need an agent for customer onboarding emails"
- "Create an agent that monitors social media mentions weekly"
- "I want a specialist who can write product descriptions for our e-commerce store"

No code required. Your new agent inherits all the business context you've already shared, so it's up to speed from the start.

## Your team grows organically

Here's where it gets interesting: your agents can create new agents for you.

When your team encounters work that would benefit from a dedicated specialist, an agent can spin up a new team member on the spot. You might ask your team to handle a new type of task, and they'll create someone to own it going forward.

This happens automatically when it makes sense, but you can also be explicit: "I need someone who handles our weekly reports" or "Create a specialist for partnership outreach." Either way, the new agent is immediately available for future work.

Your team becomes more yours over time. It adapts to the work you actually do, not just what you thought you'd need when you started. And you stay in control — you can refine any new agent's behavior through conversation, just like you would with the original team.

## Growing your team

You start with eight agents, but your team can grow as your business does. Add new specialists whenever you hit a gap in what your current team can handle.

Some businesses run lean with just the core agents. Others build out dozens of specialists for different functions. Your AI team scales with you — add what you need, when you need it.

Power users who want granular control can fine-tune agent behaviors through the web platform. But for most people, conversation is all you need.
