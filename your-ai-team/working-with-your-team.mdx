Tell your AI team what you need the same way you'd message a colleague. No special commands, no complicated workflows — just describe the task and let them handle it.

## Delegating tasks

Open Slack and message Appy.AI with what you need. Write in plain language, just like you'd ask a coworker.

Here are some examples:

- "Draft a follow-up email for the client meeting yesterday"
- "What are the SEO opportunities for our homepage?"
- "Research what our top 3 competitors are doing on social media"
- "Summarize the team standup recording and pull out action items"
- "Create a header image for our blog post about spring promotions"
- "Write a cold outreach email for the leads list I uploaded"

Need a proposal drafted or a report put together? Just tell your agent what you need and they'll deliver a formatted Word document — ready to review, edit, or send. They can include tables, images, and even pull in charts from earlier in your conversation.

Your agents can also make phone calls for you. Ask your agent to call a vendor about an invoice, check in with a contact, or follow up on a lead. They'll handle the conversation and bring you back a transcript and summary of what was discussed.

Appy.AI reads your request and routes it to the right agent automatically. You don't need to know which specialist handles what — just describe the outcome you want.

## How agents work together

Some tasks need more than one specialist. When that happens, your agents coordinate automatically behind the scenes.

Say you ask for a blog post about your new product launch. The Content Strategist drafts the article, then the Senior SEO Strategist optimizes it for search, and finally the Image Creation Specialist generates a header image. You get the finished result — not three separate handoffs.

Or imagine you're preparing for a client pitch. The Competitive Researcher pulls together intel on the prospect's market, the Brand Strategist ensures your messaging aligns with your voice, and the Content Strategist drafts the presentation outline. It all comes back to you as one cohesive deliverable.

## Autonomous work

Your AI team can work in the background without you asking. Set up schedules and triggers to handle recurring tasks automatically.

Some examples of what you can automate:

- Weekly competitive intelligence digests
- Daily summaries of industry news
- Automatic meeting summaries after every call
- Regular content performance reports

Configure autonomous tasks through triggers in each agent's settings on the web platform.

## Checking in on results

Results come back to you in Slack, right in the same thread where you made the request. Review the work and respond in that thread to iterate.

Want changes? Just say so:

- "Make it more concise"
- "Add a section about pricing"
- "Can you make the tone more casual?"

Your agents refine the work based on your feedback. Keep iterating until you're happy with the result.

Need to revisit something from last week? The web platform keeps a full history of all your conversations and completed work.
