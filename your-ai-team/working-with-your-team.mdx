# Working with Your Team

The core Appy.AI experience is simple: tell your AI team what you need the same way you'd message a colleague. They handle the rest.

## Delegating tasks

Message Appy.AI in Slack with what you need. Use plain language — no special commands or syntax required.

**Example messages you might send:**

- "Draft a follow-up email for the client meeting we had yesterday"
- "What are the SEO opportunities for our homepage?"
- "Create a social media post announcing our summer sale"
- "Research what pricing models our competitors are using"
- "Write a how-to guide for setting up our product"

You don't need to know which agent handles what. Appy.AI routes your request to the right specialist automatically. Results come back in the same conversation.

If you want to be specific, you can mention a particular agent by name. But most of the time, just describe the work and let your team figure out the best way to handle it.

## How agents work together

Some tasks need more than one specialist. When that happens, your agents coordinate automatically.

For example, if you ask "Create a blog post about our new product," here's what might happen behind the scenes:

1. The Content Strategist drafts the post
2. The SEO Strategist optimizes it for search
3. The Image Creation Specialist creates a header image

Each agent passes their work to the next. You don't manage the handoffs — you just get the final result.

This coordination happens automatically based on what the task requires. Your agents know how to work together, so you can focus on the outcome rather than the process.

## Autonomous work

Your agents don't just respond to requests — they can also run on schedules or in the background without you asking.

**Examples of autonomous work:**

- Weekly competitive research digests delivered every Monday
- Daily summaries of industry news relevant to your business
- Automatic follow-up drafts after meetings
- Regular content audits of your website

You set it up once by telling Appy.AI what you want and when. After that, results arrive automatically. This is configured through triggers in individual agent settings on the web platform.

<Info>
Autonomous work is great for recurring tasks you'd otherwise forget or deprioritize. Set it and let your team handle it.
</Info>

## Checking in on results

Results arrive in Slack, right in the conversation where you made the request. You can ask follow-up questions or request changes in the same thread.

**Iterate naturally:**

- "Make it more concise"
- "Add a section about pricing"
- "Can you make the tone more casual?"
- "Give me two more options"

Your agents remember the context of the conversation, so you don't need to repeat yourself.

The web platform also shows agent activity and history if you want to review past work or see what your team has been doing. This is useful for tracking completed tasks or picking up where you left off.
