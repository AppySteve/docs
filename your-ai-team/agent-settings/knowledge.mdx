# Knowledge

Knowledge is the reference material your agents draw on — your processes, your documentation, your way of doing things. It's what makes your AI team truly yours.

## How Knowledge Works

Upload files and your agents automatically draw on them when relevant. No extra configuration needed — agents find the right information at the right time.

Your agents don't just follow instructions — they have depth from your actual materials. This means they can answer questions, make recommendations, and complete tasks based on your specific expertise and processes.

## Real-World Examples

A coach might upload: proprietary coaching framework, workbook exercises, FAQ for common questions.

A consulting firm might upload: service descriptions, process documentation, case studies.

A business might upload: company policies, SOPs, product specs.

## Types of Knowledge Files

**Documents:** Markdown (.md), Text (.txt), PDF (.pdf)

**Images:** PNG, JPG, GIF — visual references your agents can interpret

## Adding Knowledge

### Through Conversation

Attach a file in Slack and tell Appy.AI what it is. Appy.AI uploads it and confirms.

Example messages:
- "Here's our coaching methodology guide. Add this to my agent's knowledge."
- "I'm attaching our return policy. Use this when answering questions about returns."

### Through the Web Platform

Navigate to Knowledge in the agent settings and click "Add knowledge" button. Upload files from your computer.

Files appear in the knowledge list immediately. Delete via the three-dot menu.

## Knowledge Options

**Content** — file name with type icon

**Type** — format (Markdown, Text, PDF, Image)

**Size** — file size (smaller focused files work better)

**Uploaded** — date added

## Smart References

Optional: add `@[fileName]` in agent behavior instructions to point an agent at specific files.

Examples:
- "When answering questions about returns, always consult @ReturnPolicy.md"
- "Use @CoachingFramework.md as the foundation for all coaching recommendations."

Smart references are helpful for prioritizing specific docs for certain topics. They're not required — agents access knowledge automatically without them.

<Info>
For best results, break large documents into smaller, focused files. Separate files for each policy or process work better than one massive document.
</Info>

## Getting Started

1. **Think about your expertise** — what documents contain your unique processes and knowledge?
2. **Keep files focused** — one topic per file works better than comprehensive mega-documents
3. **Upload via conversation or web** — whichever is more convenient for you
4. **Test by asking questions** — see how your agents use the knowledge in real conversations
5. **Add smart references if needed** — point agents to specific files for specific topics
