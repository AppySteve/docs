Triggers control when your agents start working. By default, agents respond when you message them — but they can also run 24/7 on cloud infrastructure, working autonomously with no maintenance required.

## Trigger types

- **On demand** — Message Appy.AI in Slack and your agent gets to work immediately. This is how most interactions happen.
- **Scheduled** — Agents can run on a recurring schedule — daily, weekly, or monthly. Great for things like morning news digests, weekly competitive reports, or monthly performance summaries.
- **Webhooks** — External tools can trigger your agents automatically. When something happens in another system — like a new lead added to your CRM — your agent can kick off and handle it.
- **Email** — Send an email to your agent's unique address and it processes the content automatically. Useful for summarizing articles or documents that come in via email.

You can combine multiple triggers on the same agent. A reporting agent might run on a weekly schedule but also be available for you to message anytime you want an ad-hoc report.

The easiest way to set up triggers is just to ask: "Send me a competitive update every Monday morning" or "Change my daily report to 6am instead of 8am."

## Recurring schedules

Your AI team doesn't only respond when you ask. With **recurring schedules**, any agent on your team can pick up the same job on a cadence you set — every weekday morning, every Friday at close of business, the first of the month — and deliver the result back to you in Slack, Teams, or wherever you work.

Think of it as the difference between asking a colleague to "remind me about this on Monday" and having a teammate who already knows it's Monday and shows up with the answer.

### What a recurring schedule is

A recurring schedule is a standing instruction you give your AI team. Once it's set up, the agent will:

- Wake up at the time you specified (in your timezone)
- Carry out the task the same way it would if you'd asked in chat
- Deliver the result to the destination you chose (a DM, a channel, or a thread)
- Repeat on the cadence you set, until you pause or remove it

The schedule runs whether your computer is on or off — Appy handles it in the cloud, so you don't have to keep an app open.

### What scheduled tasks can do

Anything an agent can do for you in chat, it can do on a schedule. That includes:

- Searching and summarizing Slack, Teams, email, and connected tools
- Pulling data from spreadsheets, Drive, CRMs, and other integrations
- Running web research and competitor scans
- Generating reports, briefs, and recap documents
- Drafting messages, posts, and outbound emails
- Producing files (PDFs, spreadsheets, images) and dropping them in chat

If a member of your AI team can do it once, you can put it on repeat.

### Where to set one up

You set up recurring schedules by asking **Violet** in chat — the same way you delegate any task.

Tell Violet what you want, when you want it, and where you want the result. Violet will pick the right agent on your team and create the schedule for you.

A few examples of how to phrase it:

- *"Every weekday at 8 AM, have Sarah send me a briefing of my calendar and any unread emails marked important."*
- *"Every Monday at 9, have Scout check what our top three competitors shipped last week and post a summary to #competitive-intel."*
- *"On the first of every month, have Lincoln pull an SEO audit on appy.ai and DM it to me."*
- *"Every Friday at 4 PM, ask Piper to surface 15 new leads matching our ICP and drop the list in #pipeline."*

You can include the destination ("DM me," "post in #channel-name"), the timezone if it isn't your default, and any task-specific context the agent needs.

If you change your mind about when, where, or how the schedule runs, just tell Violet — "stop my Monday competitive check," "move my morning briefing to 7 AM," "have it go to #leadership instead." Violet handles the update for you.

### Where to manage them

Today, **Violet is your control panel** for scheduled work. To pause, change, or remove a schedule, just say so in chat.

You can also see what's scheduled at a glance:

1. Click your avatar to open **Settings**
2. Choose the **Schedules** tab

The Schedules tab is a read-only view of every recurring schedule running on your behalf. For each one you'll see:

- A plain-English description of when it runs (e.g. "Every weekday at 9:00 AM Eastern")
- What it has been asked to do
- Where the result is delivered
- The last few runs and whether each one succeeded

To make any change to a schedule, head back to chat and ask Violet.

> **Why is everything routed through Violet?** Violet has full context on your team — which agents you have, what they're good at, and how they're connected to your tools. Letting her own the create/edit/delete flow means a schedule can't drift out of sync with the agent that runs it.

### When schedules run

- Schedules run in the timezone you specified when you set them up. Daylight saving transitions are handled automatically.
- A schedule runs in the cloud on Appy's infrastructure — you don't need to keep a browser tab or app open.
- If an agent fails a run (for example, a connected integration's authorization expired), Appy will let you know in chat so you can reconnect and resume.

### Use cases to get you started

A few patterns we see working well across teams:

**Daily briefings.** Start your day with a digest of what changed overnight — inbox highlights, calendar conflicts, channels that lit up, deals that moved.

**Weekly reports.** Roll up activity from your CRM, spreadsheets, or connected tools every Monday morning so you can walk into the week informed.

**Recurring research.** Track competitors, industry news, regulatory changes, or a topic you care about. The agent does the digging; you read the recap.

**File and inbox upkeep.** Have an agent sort attachments into the right folders, archive what's stale, or clear out one-off subscriptions on a cadence.

**Team and pipeline updates.** Standup summaries pulled from project tools, lead lists refreshed each Friday, a weekly customer-health roll-up posted to #leadership.

**Operational reminders.** End-of-quarter checklists, monthly billing pulls, a "renewals coming up in 30 days" sweep — anything you'd otherwise put on a calendar and then forget about.

### A few things to know

- **Schedules belong to you.** A schedule you create runs on your behalf, with your integrations and your delivery preferences. Other people on your team set up their own.
- **Results show up where you said.** Whether that's a Slack DM, a Slack channel, a Teams chat, or a thread — Appy routes each run to the destination you chose.
- **Pausing is the same as resuming.** If a schedule no longer makes sense, ask Violet to pause it. Pick it back up later and you don't have to rebuild it.
