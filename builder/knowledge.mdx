Knowledge is the reference material your agents draw on — your processes, your documentation, your way of doing things. It's what makes your agent sound like you and follow your methods instead of giving generic answers.

## How knowledge works

When you upload knowledge files, your agents automatically use them when relevant. There's no extra configuration needed — your agent knows to search its knowledge when answering questions or completing tasks.

This means your agent doesn't just follow instructions. It has depth. It can reference your actual methodologies, follow your real procedures, and provide the kind of specific, informed answers that only someone with your background could give.

## Real-world examples

Here's how different businesses use knowledge:

**A coach** might upload:
- Their proprietary coaching framework
- A library of exercises and reflection prompts
- An FAQ covering common client questions

**A consulting firm** might upload:
- Service descriptions and pricing guides
- Process documentation and delivery templates
- Case study summaries for reference

**A business** might upload:
- Company policies and employee handbooks
- Standard operating procedures (SOPs)
- Product specifications or technical documentation

The key is uploading the materials that capture how you work and what makes your approach unique.

## Types of knowledge files

**Documents** — Written content your agent can read and reference. This includes methodology guides, policies, how-to instructions, FAQs, research summaries, or any text-based material.

Supported formats: Markdown (.md), Text (.txt), PDF (.pdf)

**Images** — Visual references your agent can interpret. This includes diagrams, charts, infographics, screenshots, or photos that help your agent understand and describe visual concepts.

Supported formats: PNG, JPG, GIF

## Adding knowledge

You have two ways to add knowledge to your agents.

### Through conversation

The fastest way is through your conversation with Appy.AI in Slack. Simply attach a file and tell Appy.AI what it is.

Try saying:
- "Here's our coaching methodology guide. Add this to my agent's knowledge."
- "I'm attaching our company's return policy. My agent should use this when answering questions about returns."
- "Add this product catalog image to my agent's knowledge so it can reference our current lineup."

Appy.AI will upload the file and confirm it's been added. You can keep building without switching tools.

### Through the web platform

You can also manage knowledge directly through the web platform:

1. Navigate to your agent's settings
2. Click **Knowledge** in the left navigation
3. Click the **Add knowledge** button
4. Select files from your computer to upload
5. Your files will appear in the knowledge list immediately

To remove knowledge:
1. Find the file in your knowledge list
2. Click the **three-dot menu** next to the file
3. Select **Delete**

Your knowledge list shows each file's name, type, size, and upload date so you can easily manage what your agent has access to.

## Knowledge options

When you view your knowledge list, you'll see these details for each file:

**Content** — The file name and icon representing its type

**Type** — The format of the file (PDF, Markdown, Image, etc.)

**Size** — The file size. Smaller files work better — consider breaking large documents into focused, single-topic files.

**Uploaded** — The date the file was added to your agent's knowledge

<Info>
Break large documents into smaller, focused files for better results. For example, separate files for each policy or each chapter work better than one massive document.
</Info>

## Smart references

Your agent automatically searches its knowledge when responding, so in most cases you don't need to do anything extra. Just upload your files and your agent will use them.

For more control, you can add smart references in your agent's behavior instructions using the `@[fileName]` format. This tells your agent to prioritize specific knowledge files for certain topics.

Here are some examples:
- "When answering questions about returns, always consult @ReturnPolicy for the current policy."
- "Use @CoachingFramework as the foundation for all coaching recommendations."
- "Reference @PricingGuide when users ask about service tiers or costs."

Smart references are helpful when you want your agent to consistently prioritize a particular document for certain topics. They're optional — your agent will find and use knowledge automatically without them.

## Getting started

Here's how to start building your agent's knowledge:

1. **Think about what makes you the expert** — What documents, guides, or materials capture your unique approach?

2. **Keep files focused** — Smaller, single-topic files work better than one large document covering everything.

3. **Upload through Slack or the web platform** — Use whichever method is easier in the moment.

4. **Test your agent** — Ask questions that should draw on your expertise to make sure it responds the way you would.

5. **Add smart references if needed** — If you want your agent to prioritize specific files for certain topics, add `@[fileName]` references in your behavior instructions.

<Info>
Start with your most important documents first. You can always add more knowledge over time as you see what your agent needs.
</Info>
