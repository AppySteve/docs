Behavior settings control how an individual agent responds — its tone, approach, and the instructions it follows. This is where you shape your agent's personality and give it the guidelines it needs to handle conversations and work the way you want.

## How behavior works

Think of behavior as your agent's personality and playbook. It's the combination of guidelines that determine how your agent handles conversations and completes work.

When someone interacts with your agent, it draws on these behavior settings to decide how to respond, what tone to use, and what approach to take. You're not programming logic — you're giving your agent a clear sense of how you want it to show up.

## What you can configure

You have full control over how your agent behaves. Here's what you can adjust:

**Tone and voice** — Set whether your agent is casual and friendly or formal and professional. You can make it warm and conversational for customer support, or direct and data-focused for analytics work.

**Task approach** — Guide how your agent tackles work. Some agents work best with step-by-step instructions, while others need to see the big picture first and figure out the details.

**Specific rules** — Add guidelines like "always include a call-to-action at the end" or "never make assumptions about pricing — always ask first." These rules help your agent stay consistent.

**Domain-specific instructions** — If your agent handles specialized work, you can include industry terminology, preferred formats, or specific processes it should follow.

## Configuring behavior

You have two ways to configure your agent's behavior, and you can use both depending on what's easier in the moment.

**Through conversation with Appy.AI in Slack** — This is the easiest way. Just tell Appy.AI what you want changed, and it updates your agent's behavior for you.

Try saying things like:
- "Make my support agent more friendly and casual"
- "My agent should always respond in 3 paragraphs or less"
- "Add a rule that the agent should never discuss pricing without checking with me first"

**Through the web platform's agent settings** — If you prefer direct control, you can edit your agent's behavior instructions manually through the web interface. Navigate to your agent's settings, find the Behavior section, and write or adjust the instructions yourself.

<Info>
Both methods work equally well — use whichever feels more natural for the change you're making.
</Info>

## Smart references in behavior

You can reference knowledge files directly in your behavior instructions using the `@[fileName]` format. This tells your agent to prioritize specific files when handling certain topics.

For example:
- "When handling returns, follow @ReturnPolicy.md"
- "Use @CoachingFramework.pdf as the foundation for all coaching advice"
- "Reference @PricingGuide.txt when discussing service tiers"

Smart references are optional — your agent will automatically search its knowledge when needed. But if you want to make sure it always uses a specific file for certain situations, smart references give you that control.

## Tips for better behavior

**Start basic, refine over time** — Don't try to write the perfect behavior instructions on day one. Start with the essentials and adjust as you see how your agent performs.

**Be specific** — Instead of "be concise," say "respond in 3 paragraphs or less." Instead of "be helpful," say "always offer two options when someone asks for recommendations."

**Test by asking questions** — After updating behavior, test it by asking your agent questions or giving it tasks. See if it responds the way you expect. If not, adjust and test again.

<Info>
The best way to dial in your agent's behavior is to use it yourself first. Spend time interacting with it, note what feels off, and make small adjustments until it feels right.
</Info>
